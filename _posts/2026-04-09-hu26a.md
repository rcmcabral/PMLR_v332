title: Annotator Risk Preference as a Catalyst for Systemic Bias in Multimodal AI
abstract: As artificial intelligence evolves toward multimodal cognition, systems
  are moving beyond unimodal dependencies to integrate visual, auditory, and linguistic
  dimensions, thereby simulating human perception of reality. However, this increased
  complexity not only enhances expressiveness but also opens more insidious channels
  for bias infiltration. Existing research largely focuses on the demographic attributes
  of annotators (e.g., race, gender) while overlooking critical variables within the
  dimension of decision psychology (Ferrara, 2024; Sap et al., 2022). Among these,
  risk preference acts as a core driver of individual decision-making, exerting a
  subtle anchoring effect during the multimodal annotation process. When annotators
  confront materials characterized by high ambiguity, fuzziness, or potential social
  sensitivity, their intrinsic risk tolerance directly dictates label polarity, the
  degree of neutralization, and sensitivity toward minority attributes.
